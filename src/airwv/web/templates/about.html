About AirWV

Open-source, community-run air-quality monitoring for West Virginia.

What this is

Empower WV — community eco monitoring collects readings from community PurpleAir sensors across West Virginia, stores long-term history, and surfaces trends, anomalies, and how those sensors compare against government reference monitors. It grew out of the Kanawha Valley Air Quality Monitoring project (Create WV, WVCAG, and partners).

Where the data comes from

  • Community sensors — low-cost PurpleAir monitors run by residents and partner organizations. Great coverage and responsiveness; calibrated against reference monitors before you lean on the numbers.
  • EPA reference monitors — regulatory-grade data pulled directly and keyless from EPA AirNow (live) and EPA AirData/AQS (deep history). We use these to validate the community sensors.
  • Pollution-source context — permitted facilities from public records (EPA TRI and others), shown factually and cited, never as an accusation.

Full detail: DATA-SOURCES.md. We reached the reference data through OpenAQ first — thanks to them and their open tooling.

Provisional, not regulatory

Readings are shown as-is for community awareness. Community sensors are not regulatory instruments; treat the data as a helpful signal, not an official determination. For health decisions during smoke or pollution events, also check AirNow.gov.

Open source — help out

Everything here is open. Read the code, file an issue, run your own instance, or add a sensor. Repo & docs: github.com/createwv/airwv.
